A complete step-by-step journey from beginner to job-ready data analyst.

## 1. Mathematics & Statistics Fundamentals

### Why It Matters
You need these to understand trends, outliers, probability, and the story behind your data.  
Without this, even advanced dashboards or ML models won‚Äôt make sense.

### What to Learn
- Descriptive Statistics: Mean, Median, Mode, Range, Variance, Standard Deviation  
- Probability Basics: Independent & dependent events, permutations, combinations  
- Distributions: Normal, Binomial, Poisson  
- Hypothesis Testing: Null vs Alternate, p-value, confidence intervals  
- Correlation & Regression: Simple & multiple linear regression

- YouTube (Krish Naik): *Complete Statistics For Data Science in 6 Hours*(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LZzq1zSL1bs)
- Book (Optional): *Practical Statistics for Data Scientists* ‚Äì Bruce & Gedeck

## 3. SQL (Structured Query Language)

### Why It Matters
SQL is the language of data.  
Every analyst uses it daily to pull, join, and transform data from databases.

### What to Learn (80/20 Focus)
- SELECT, WHERE, ORDER BY, GROUP BY, HAVING  
- JOINS (INNER, LEFT, RIGHT, FULL)  
- Subqueries & CTEs  
- Aggregate Functions (SUM, COUNT, AVG, MIN, MAX)  
- Window Functions (ROW_NUMBER, RANK, LAG, LEAD)  
- CASE WHEN & Conditional Logic  

### Resources
- YouTube Channel: [TechTFQ](https://www.youtube.com/@techTFQ/videos) (best free SQL course)  
- LeetSQL (practice problems)
- https://github.com/SahilGogna/Study-Resources/blob/main/analysis/sql.md

## 4. Data Visualization Tools

### Why It Matters
You need to communicate insights visually.  
Learning one visualization tool deeply is enough to showcase in your portfolio.

### What to Learn

Then learn:
- Importing and Cleaning Data  
- Creating Bar, Line, Pie, Map, and KPI charts  
- Calculated Fields and Parameters  
- Dashboard Design & Storytelling  
- Publishing and Sharing Reports
